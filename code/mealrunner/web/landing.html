1.

Plan in your own shorthand

Type the meals your family already knows — "tacos," "burgers," "stir fry." No recipe browsing, no scrolling through someone else's dinners.

2.

Grocery list builds itself

MealRunner turns each meal into the ingredients you told it you needed. Add staples once and they roll forward every week.

3.

Only the staples you actually need

MealRunner skips the tiny pantry stuff — salt, oil, black pepper — so you're not staring at twenty items you already have. Recipe ingredients still land on the list; you flag the small things only when you actually need a refill.

4.

Order through Kroger or scan the receipt

One-tap ordering when you shop Kroger, or upload a receipt from any store and MealRunner reconciles what you actually bought.

Buy smarter as you go.

MealRunner surfaces the pricing and product details you'd usually have to hunt for. You still pick what to buy — you just have more to go on.

$

Cheaper equivalents as you order

When you pick a Kroger product, MealRunner shows same-category, same-size alternatives that cost less per unit. You decide if they're worth swapping.

↻

Reuse ingredients across the week

Tap Optimize and MealRunner suggests small swaps — a different meal, or one ingredient inside a meal — so a single purchase can carry two dinners instead of one.

◎

See what's really in your cart

NOVA processing scores, Nutri-Score, and the parent company behind each brand — surfaced next to the products you're about to buy.

↗

Know what your groceries actually cost

MealRunner quietly logs prices from your orders and receipts. Over a few weeks you see what your typical basket costs and when it tends to be cheapest.
